Skin Types & Diseases

Actinic Keratosis

A precancerous skin condition that appears as rough, dry, or scaly patches. It is mainly caused by long-term exposure to ultraviolet (UV) sunlight and commonly develops on sun-exposed areas like face, ears, and hands.

Atopic Dermatitis

A chronic inflammatory skin disease that causes redness, itching, dryness, and irritation. It often begins in childhood and may flare up due to allergens, stress, or environmental factors.

Benign Keratosis

A non-cancerous skin growth that usually appears as a brown or black rough patch. It has a waxy or wart-like appearance and generally does not require medical treatment.

Dermatofibroma

A harmless, firm skin nodule commonly found on the arms or legs. It may develop after minor skin injuries like insect bites and usually feels hard when touched.

Melanocytic Nevus

Commonly known as a mole, this is a benign growth formed by melanocytes. These can be flat or raised and vary in color, usually harmless unless changes in size, shape, or color occur.

Melanoma

A serious and aggressive form of skin cancer that develops from pigment- producing cells. It often appears as an irregular mole and can spread quickly if not detected early.

Squamous Cell Carcinoma

A common type of skin cancer that appears as scaly red patches, open sores, or raised growths. It is mainly caused by prolonged sun exposure and may bleed or crust over time.

Tinea Ringworm

A contagious fungal infection characterized by circular, ring-shaped rashes. It causes itching, redness, and scaling and spreads through skin contact or shared personal items.

Vascular Lesion

A skin abnormality caused by irregular blood vessels, appearing as red, purple, or blue marks on the skin. These lesions are usually harmless but can vary in size and shape.
